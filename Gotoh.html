The Gotoh (1982) algorithm by Osamu Gotoh computes the optimal, global affine alignment of two sequences. The problem with Needleman-Wunsch (1970) was that it does not produce biologically relevant alignments. Alignments had too many gaps and by this reason, the Gotoh algorithm was introduced. In this algorithm, the number of gap starts is minimized to get biologically better alignments.

This done by introducing two additional matrices $P$ and $Q$ (which are storing the vertical and horizontal gap costs) and a new recursion function $D_{i,j}$ (w.r.t. Needleman-Wunsch). The asymptotic runtime and space consumption is however, not higher than in the Needleman-Wunsch algorithm. Runtime and space are in $\mathcal O(nm)$, where $n=|S_1|$ and $m=|S_2|$.
