Different agglomerative (hierarchical) clustering algorithms were proposed to compute phylogenetic trees from evolutionary distances:
  • UPGMA (average linkage) by Robert R. Sokal and Charles D. Michener (1958)
  • WPGMA (simple average) by Louis L. McQuitty (1967)
  • neighbour-joining (single linkage) by Naruya Saitou and Masatoshi Nei (1987)
The principle is always the same, you have a given a matrix of pairwise evolutionary distances between singleton clusters (containing only one element) and in this matrix you search for the entry with the lowest value $d_{min} = d_{i,j}$ (where $i \neq j$). You merge the clusters $i$ and $j$ to a new cluster $ij = i \cup j$ and recompute the distances to the new cluster $ij = i \cup j$ with some distance formula $dist$ given with the approach.
